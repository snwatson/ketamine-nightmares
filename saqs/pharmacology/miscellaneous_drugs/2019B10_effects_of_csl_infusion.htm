·         Metabolic effects

 

Summary:

·         Significant initial plasma expansion, subsequent distribution to ISF > plasma > ICF

·         Distribution and excretion may depend upon hydration status

·         Multi-system response restores plasma volume to normal

·         Causes less electrolyte derangement than normal saline

Fluid effects:

·         Immediate distribution

o   Entire 2L into plasma (20% ↑blood volume)

o   Distribution half life ~5 minutes

·         Steady state distribution

o   479mL in plasma (10% ↑ volume)

o   1438mL in ISF (14% ↑ volume)

o   83mL in ICF (minimal %↑ volume)

·         Excretion:

o   Glomerulotubular imbalance

§  ↓Oncotic pressure -> ↑GFR + ↓prox tubule reabsorption -> ↑urine flow rate

o   Pressure diuresis

§  ↑mAP -> ↑urine output (? mechanism)

o   Hormonal response

§  ↓ADH

§  ↓RAAS

§  ↑ANP

(Note excretion of both water and Na+ is faster than for normal saline)

 

Metabolic effects:

·         Acid-base:

o   Metabolic alkalosis (wins)

§  58mmol lactate infused -> 58mM H+ consumed

§  Lactate + H+ -> CO2 + H2O

§  58mmol lactate infused -> 58mmol H+ consumed (≈ 58mmol HCO3- produced)

o   Metabolic acidosis (loses)

§  ↑[Cl-] -> ↓strong ion difference -> ↑dissociation of H2O -> ↑[H+]

·         Hypothermia:

o   Effect: ↓core temp and skin temp (? >1°C)

o   Response: vasoconstriction, shivering, heat conservation behaviour

·         Osmolality:

o   ↓2mM -> ↓<1% -> no response (ADH sensitivity ∆3%)

 

Cardiovascular effects:

·         Direct effects

o   ↑Blood volume -> distension of capacitance vessels -> ↑MSFP -> ↑venous return -> ↑preload -> ↑cardiac output -> ↑mAP

·         Response

o   High pressure baroreceptors

§  ↑mAP -> ↑stretch -> ↑afferents to NTS -> ↓SNS output, ↑PSNS output -> ↓HR, ↓contractility, vasodilatation

o   Low pressure baroreceptors

§  ↑CVP -> ↑stretch -> ↑afferents to NTS -> same

 

Neuroendocrine effects:

·         RAAS

o   ↑mAP -> ↑renal baroreceptor stretch -> ↓renin release -> ↓angiotensin 2

o   Vasodilatation

o   ↓Na+/H+/H2O reabsorption from proximal tubule

·         ADH

o   ↑Venoatrial stretch -> ↓ADH release (sensitivity ~∆10%)

o   Vasodilatation

o   AQP2 downregulation -> ↓H2O reabsorption from collecting ducts

o   Ureaporin downregulation -> ↓medullary interstitial osmolality -> ↓H2O reabsorption

·         ANP

o   ↑CVP -> ↑venoatrial stretch -> ↑ANP release

o   Dilatation of afferent arterioles -> ↑GFR, ↑vasa recta flow, washout of medullary interstitium

o   Inhibit NCC in distal tubule -> ↓Na+Cl- reabsorption

o   Inhibit ENaC in collecting ducts -> ↓Na+ reabsorption
