Pre-eclampsia

 

Disease:

Definition

1. Hypertension:

·      >140/90 after 20 weeks

2. End-organ dysfunction

·      Placenta: IUGR

·      Kidney: proteinuria >30mg/mmol, ↑creat

·      Liver: ↑transaminases, RUQ pain

·      Haem: ↓plt, haemolysis, DIC

·      Neuro: hyperreflexia, confusion, seizure, stroke

Risk factors

Obvious:

·      PHx

·      FHx

Obstetric:

·      Previous pre-eclampsia

·      Nulliparous, or grand multiparous

·      Donor egg, or donor embryos

Comorbidities:

·      Older

·      Metabolic (obesity, HTN, T2DM, CKD)

·      Autoimmune (APLS, other)

Pathophysiology

·      Abnormal placentation

·      Endothelial funny business

·      Organ dysfunction

Pre-eclampsia issues: baby

Abnormal placentation

·      IUGR

Premature delivery

·      Neurotoxicity

·      Poor thermoregulation (warm)

·      Lung and resp centre immaturity (pink)

·      Poor glycogen storage and feeding (sweet)

 

Goals: mother

Stop the disease

·      Deliver!

o   If disease severe

o   If baby ready (>37/40)

No stroke

·      Aim <160/100

Outpatient:

·      Methyldopa

·      Labetalol

·      Nifedipine

·      Hydralazine

·      Prazosin

·      Clonidine

In theatre:

·      Labetalol 5-10mg q2 min max 200mg

·      Hydralazine 5-10mg q15 min max 20mg

No seizure

·      MgSO4 4g 15min + 1g/h for 24h

No haemorrhage

·      Blood tests: FBE, coag, BGHO

·      Oxytocin: immediately after delivery

·      Ergometrine: avoid, but do not with-hold if severe PPH

No paralysis

·      Blood tests pre-op

·      Plt >80: spinal

·      Plt 50-80: pros vs cons. Consider trend.

·      Plt <50: GA

No drowning

·      IDC for monitoring

·      Titrate IV fluid to UO 0.5mL/kg/h

·      Concentrated oxytocin infusion

 

Goals: foetus

No asphyxia

·      Avoid maternal hypotension too. Aim mAP >70

·      Continuous CTG

·      Paediatrician present for birth

No lung injury

·      Delay delivery if safe

·      Glucocorticoids if <35/40

No brain injury

·      MgSO4 if <32/40

 

GA Caesarean recipe for pre-eclampsia

Pre-theatre

·      Treatment: Mg2+, BP control

·      BP control

·      Check blood tests: plt, coag, BGHO

·      Call O&G consultant and paediatrician – be there

·      Call theatre: A-line, fluid warmer, bair hugger

Pre-induction

·      Continue treatment

·      A-line

·      2 x IVC

Induction

·      mRSI

·      Alf 20mcg/kg, prop 3mg/kg, sux 1.5mg/kg

·      Topicalise glottis with 2mL 10% lignocaine MAD

o   Allow awake extubation without stimulation

·      Video laryngoscope

Maintenance

·      More opioid

·      More antihypertensives

·      TXA for bleeding (beware seizure post-op)

·      Concentrated oxytocin after delivery

·      No drowning (rationalize IV fluid)

·      No ergotamine (stroke)

·      No tramadol (seizure)

·      No NSAID (renal failure)
