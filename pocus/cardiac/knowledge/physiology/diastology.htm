Diastology

 

Mitral inflow velocity:

Description

·      Rate of flow into the ventricle during different phases of diastole

Measurements

·      E: flow rate during early diastole

·      A: flow rate during atrial systole

·      DT: deceleration time

How to

·      Apical 4 chamber view

·      PW doppler at the tips of the MV leaflets (?)

Interpretation

·      If E>A and normal appearance: probably normal heart

·      If E>A and abnormal appearance: probably abnormal heart

·      If E<A and older patient: to be expected

 

Mitral annular velocity:

Description

·      e’: rate of expansion of the ventricle during early diastole

·      E/e’: relationship between blood entering and ventricle expanding
(i.e. compliance)

Measurements

·      e’: velocity at the mitral valve annulus

o   Septal (tends to overcall diastolic dysfunction)

o   Lateral

o   Average

How to

·      Apical 4 chamber view

·      TDI at the MV annulus – septal and lateral

 

Pulmonary vein flow velocity:

Description

·      Rate (and direction) of flow of blood into the left atrium during both diastole and systole

Measurements

·      S: ventricular systole

o   S1: atrial relaxation

o   S2: atrial elongation due to annular descent

·      D: diastole (early phase and diastasis)

·      A: atrial systole causing retrograde flow

How to

·      Apical 4 chamber view

·      PW doppler in the right upper pulmonary vein (?)

·      E/e’ > 14

·      Septal e’ <7cm/s

·      TR velocity >2.8m/s (why??)

·      LA volume index >34mL/m2

Output

·      0-1 point: normal

·      2 points: indeterminate

·      3-4 points: abnormal
