Conduction delay:

 

SA node

·     Sinus arrhythmia: mild respiratory oscillation in HR

·     Sick sinus syndrome: major variation in HR

AV node

·     1st: long PR interval (>0.2msec 5 small squares)

·     2nd: some conduction

o   Mobitz 1: progressive ↑PR then dropped QRS (Wenckebach)

o   Mobitz 2: dropped QRS at regular interval

·     3rd: no conduction. “P waves marching through”

Significant:

·     1st degree if very long

·     2nd degree if Mobitz 2

·     3rd degree always

Bundles

·     LBBB: wide QRS, STEMI-like V1, ST depression + T inversion laterally

      

·     RBBB: wide QRS, bunny ear V1 (wide second ear)

·     LAFB: left axis deviation + qR laterally + rS inferiorly

·     LPFB: right axis deviation + rS laterally + qR inferiorly

Bifascicular block:

·     RBBB + LAFB (look for it if left axis)

·     RBBB + LPFB (look for it if right axis)

Trifascicular block:

·     AV block + RBBB + LAFB or LPFB

Significance:

·     RBBB: often normal, can indicate RV problem

·     LBBB: always abnormal (? ischaemia ? structural heart disease)

·     New LBBB = STEMI

·     Hemiblock: ok if asymptomatic

·     Bifascicular block: ok if asymptomatic

·     Trifascicular block: always abnormal

Escape rhythms

·     Junctional: narrow complex, rate 40-60

·     Ventricular: very wide complex, rate 20-40 (a.k.a. idioventricular)

·     P waves may be present and “marching through”
