2019B10 Discuss the physiological responses to administration of 2 litres of Hartmann’s solution intravenously over 1 hour to a conscious, healthy, euvolaemic 70kg adult.

 

List:

·         Summary

·         Composition

·         Distribution

·         Cardiovascular effects

·         Neuroendocrine effects

·         Metabolic effects

 

Summary:

·         Initial intravascular expansion

·         Subsequent distribution to ICF > ISF > plasma

·         Distribution may depend upon hydration state

o   If hypovolaemic: more into plasma

o   If hypervolaemic: more into urine

·         Multi-system response restores plasma volume to normal

Distribution:

·         Initially in plasma

o   1L into plasma -> 20% increase in blood volume

o   Distribution half life 5.5 minutes (completion by 28 minutes)

·         Subsequent

o   ~425mL in plasma

o   ~1300mL in ISF (no oncotic pressure)

o   ~275mL in ICF (since hypoosmolar)

 

Cardiovascular effects:

·         Direct effects

o   ↑Blood volume -> distension of capacitance vessels -> ↑MSFP -> ↑venous return -> ↑preload -> ↑cardiac output -> ↑mAP

·         Response

o   High pressure baroreceptors

§  ↑mAP -> ↑stretch -> ↑afferents to NTS -> ↓SNS output from RVLM, ↑PSNS output -> ↓HR, ↓contractility, vasodilatation

o   Low pressure baroreceptors

§  ↑CVP -> ↑stretch -> ↑afferents to NTS …

o   Renal effect?

§  Glomerulotubular balance….

 

Neuroendocrine effects:

·         RAAS

o   ↑mAP -> ↑renal baroreceptor stretch -> ↓renin release -> ↓angiotensin 2, ↓aldosterone

o   Vasodilatation

o   ↓Na+/H+/H2O reabsorption from proximal tubule

·         ADH

o   ↑Venoatrial stretch -> ↓ADH release (threshold ~∆10% cf. osmolality ~∆3%)

o   AQP2 downregulation -> ↓H2O reabsorption from collecting ducts -> diuresis

·         ANP

o   ↑CVP -> ↑venoatrial stretch -> ↑ANP release

o   Dilatation of afferent arterioles -> ↑GFR, ↑vasa recta flow, washout of medullary interstitium

o   Inhibit NCC in distal tubule -> ↓Na+Cl- reabsorption

o   Inhibit ENaC in collecting ducts -> ↓Na+ reabsorption
