Anaphylaxis

 

Recognise it:

Likely culprit

·     Relaxant

·     Antibiotic

·     Chlorhex

Likely features

·     Distributive shock (often severe)

·     Urticarial rash (absent if shocked)

·     Bronchospasm (rarely severe)

·     Upper airway swelling (rarely severe)

 

Treat it:

Priorities

·     Oxygen

·     Fluid

·     Adrenaline

1.Call for help

·     Buzzer

·     Anaphylaxis box

2.Resuscitation

1.Oxygen:

·     Supplement

·     ±Intubate

2.Fluid:

·     CSL+++

3.Adrenaline:

·     IM adrenaline 500mcg vs infusion

·     Grade 2: IV 20mcg q1-2min

·     Grade 3: IV 100-200mcg q1-2min +

·     Grade 4: ALS probably

Other:

·     Drugs: hydrocortisone, antihistamine

·     Access: A-line, CVC, IDC

·     Test: tryptase 1h, 4h, 24h

5.Cancel (probably)

Effects of anaphylaxis:

·     Fluid shifts

·     Vasoplegia

·     Coagulopathy

·     Organ failure

·     Impaired healing

(i.e. not back to normal once BP restored)

Considerations:

·     Urgency of procedure?

·     Magnitude of procedure?

·     Severity of anaphylaxis?

6.Follow up

·     Tryptase 1h, 4h, 24h

·     Referral to anaesthetic allergy clinic for skin prick testing

·     Document allergies in medical records

·     Alert document for patient to carry
