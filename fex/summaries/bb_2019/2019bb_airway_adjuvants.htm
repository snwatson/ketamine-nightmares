Flexible bougies, introducers and bronchoscopes

 

Introduction:

Classification

·      Channelled vs non-channelled

·      Macintosh-shaped vs hyperangulated 60° (Glidescope, C-MAC D Blade, McGrath)

Use of hyperangulated blades

·      ED and ICU

·      Teaching tool (I think they meant Macintosh-shaped video laryngoscope…)

·      Difficult airways: seeing around corners

Johnny’s wisdom

·      Best view does not mean easiest intubation (i.e. pull scope back a bit)

·      Technique with hyperangulated blade is very different

·      Emergency airway is not the time to try a new technique

 

Where ETTs get stuck:

Arytenoids

·      Especially with non-tapered ETT

·      Suggest Fastrach, Parker Flex-tip, Blockbuster

Anterior tracheal wall

·      Especially with hyperangulated blade

·      Suggest flexible-tipped bougie

 

Introducers:

Stylets

·      Rigid vs malleable vs flexible

·      Helps displace or elevate supraglottic structures

·      Helps get past vocal cords

·      But often gets stuck on the anterior tracheal wall and may cause trauma

Bougies

·      Good if no room

·      Flexible tipped bougie obviates anterior tracheal wall impingement

 

Novel techniques:

Double trouble

·      Assisting anaesthetist holds a hyperangulated blade

·      Intubating anaesthetist holds a flexible bronchoscope loaded with an ETT

·      Makes more room

·      Allows spontaneous ventilation

·      Facilitates suction

Try before you buy

·      Topical lignocaine

·      Have a look with a hyperangulated blade

·      Can do while spont vent GA before paralysing

·      Can do while awake before inducing anaesthesia
