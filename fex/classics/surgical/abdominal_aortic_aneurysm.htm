Elective abdominal aortic aneurysm

 

Pre-op:

Comorbidities

Problems:

·      IHD, cardiomyopathy, HTN

·      Smoking, COPD

·      T2DM

·      CKD

Response:

·      Investigations:

o  Blood: FBE, UEC, LFT, coag, HbA1c

o  Imaging: CXR, TTE

o  Stress testing

·      Review: PMU ± specialty team

·      Optimisation: lifestyle + medications + fluid status

Medications

·      Antiplatelets: cessation (except aspirin)

·      Anticoagulants: cessation ± reversal

·      Vasoactives: withhold ACE-i

M&M discussion

Major risks:

·      Spinal cord ischaemia

·      Dialysis-dependence

·      Death

Response:

·      Explore patient’s values

·      Stratify risk

·      Discuss goals of care

·      Book ICU bed

 

Intra-op:

 

Rupture prevention

Goals:

·      ↓↔HR

·      ↓↔SBP

Practice:

·      Opioid

·      Beta blocker

Bleeding

Problems: loss of

·      Blood

·      Water

·      Heat

Preparation:

·      Blood: cross-match 10 units

·      Access: 2 x big IVC + pump sets + A-line + CVC

·      Warming: Forced air warmer + fluid warmer

Monitoring:

·      Heat: nasal or bladder temp probe

·      Blood and water: PPV + ABG + viscoelastic

Goals:

·      See massive bleeding document

Clamping

·      Communication ++

1.       ↑Afterload

o  Stingy with fluid beforehand

o  Start vasodilator (GTN)

o  Aim low-ish mAP

2.       Ischaemia

o  ∝ Clamp time

o  Lower limb forced air warmer off

Unclamping

·      Communication ++

1.       ↓↓Afterload

o  Generous with fluid beforehand

o  Vasoconstrictor started (noradrenaline)

o  Aim high-ish mAP

2.       Reperfusion ++

o  ∝ Clamp time

o  ± Ask for staged unclamping

o  Lower limb forced air warmer back on

 

Post-op:

 

Pain

Neuraxial:

·      ±Thoracic epidural

·      ±ITM

Regional:

·      Bilateral rectus sheath catheters

Parenteral:

·      Ketamine infusion

·      Opioid infusion in ICU -> PCA on the ward

Monitoring:

·      APS review q12h

·      Test platelets + coags before removal of epidural catheter

Organ failure

Mitigation and prevention:

·      Vasoplegia: CVC + noradrenaline

·      Renal failure: fluid status + perfusion pressure

·      Coagulopathy: viscoelastic + warmth + products + adjuvants

·      Liver failure: not sure

·      Spinal cord ischaemia: not sure

 

Ruptured AAA

 

Anaesthesia issues:

Priorities

Timing:

·      Big rupture: surgical control before resus

·      Small rupture: resus before surgical control

Blood pressure:

·      Big rupture: avoid sympatholysis to prevent death

·      Small rupture: ensure sympatholysis to prevent pop

Aspiration risk:

·      Risk of CVS collapse is greater

·      Mitigate with 1.2mg/kg rocuronium + apnoea

Logistics

·      Anaesthesia + vascular access + massive transfusion

·      Need second anaesthetist

Induction collapse

·      Cardiac depressant

·      Sympatholytic

·      Relaxant -> release of tamponade

·      IPPV -> impedance to venous return

Massive bleeding

·      See massive bleeding document

 

Anaesthesia plan:

Access

·      Large PIVC + pump set + fluid warmer

·      Arterial line pre-induction if possible

·      CVC when possible

Monitoring

·      Standard

·      Arterial line pre-induction if possible

·      IDC when possible

Drugs

·      Crystalloid 250mL

·      Home-made adrenaline infusion (3mg in 50mL)

·      Ketamine 10-50mg until dissociated

·      Rocuronium 1.2mg/kg when dissociated

·      Sevoflurane 0.5%
