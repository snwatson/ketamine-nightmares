Proximal Isovelocity Surface Area (PISA) calculations:

Purpose

·  Estimate a) area of an unmeasurable orifice b) amount of flow through it

·  Used to quantify valvular regurgitation or stenosis

Diagramme

Description

·  Characteristic pattern of blood flow through a circular orifice

·  Organisation into concentric shells of increasing velocity
(a.k.a. proximal flow convergence zone)

·  Narrow, high velocity jet at the orifice

·  Narrowest part of the jet (vena contracta) is just distal to the orifice

·  The basis of calculations is the conservation of mass
(i.e. same amount of blood must flow at each point)

Calculation

Inputs:

·  PISA shell radius (radius of hemisphere in colour doppler)

·  Aliasing velocity (PWD at edge of hemisphere)

·  Maximum velocity (CWD through orifice)

Outputs:

·  Orifice area

·  Vena contracta

·  Regurgitant volume

Equations:

1. Area of hemisphere = 2πr2

2. Flow rate = area of hemisphere x aliasing velocity

3. Orifice area = flow rate / Vmax

4. Regurgitant volume = orifice area x velocity time integral

Limitations

·  Its time-consuming

·  Its complicated

·  Difficulty measuring dimensions accurately

·  Difficulty measuring flow rate at the correct location

·  Calculations are predicated on the orifice being circular
