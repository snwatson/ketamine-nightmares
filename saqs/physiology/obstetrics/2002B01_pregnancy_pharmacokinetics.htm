2002B01 Outline the influences of pregnancy on pharmacokinetics.

 

List:

·      Absorption

·      Distribution

·      Metabolism

·      Excretion

 

Absorption:

Oral

·  ↓Gastric emptying in labour -> ↑risk aspiration

Inhaled

·  ↑VA (progesterone on respiratory centre), ↓FRC (diaphragm compression) -> ↑rate of volatile wash-in

·  (partly offset by ↑cardiac output)

SC/IM

·  ↑blood flow -> ↑absorption rate (e.g. IM morphine in labour)

 

Distribution:

Cardiac output

·  ↑CO due to ↑preload, ↓afterload, ↑heart rate

·  ↑Rate of onset suxamethonium

·  ↑Rate of offset by distribution of propofol -> risk of awareness

·  Slows onset of volatile anaesthetic (but ↑VA:FRC ratio is more significant)

Blood

·  ↓Albumin -> ↑free acidic drug (e.g. barbiturate) (note same total amount albumin)

·  ↓AAG -> ↑free basic drug (e.g. morphine)

·  Respiratory alkalosis -> ↑% unionized basic drugs, ↓%unionised acidic drug

·  ↑Blood volume -> ↑VD plasma-confined drugs e.g. heparins

Tissues

·  ↑Muscle mass, ↑fat mass, oedema, ↑body water

·  Hence ↑VD most drugs -> ↑t1/2β most drugs

 

Metabolism and excretion:

Liver

·  Unchanged hepatic blood flow and liver function

·  Progesterone -> CYP enzyme induction

·  Oestrogen -> CYP enzyme competition

·  ↓[Plasma cholinesterase] but unchanged amount

Kidney

·  ↑Renal blood flow 80% -> ↑GFR -> ↑excretion unchanged drugs e.g. LMWH
