2019B11 Describe the respiratory and cardiovascular changes associated with a carbon dioxide pneumoperitoneum for laparoscopic surgery.

 

List:

·         Intro

·         Effects of intra-abdominal hypertension

·         Effects of hypercapnoea

·         Effects of position change

·         Miscellaneous complications

 

Intro:

·         Carbon dioxide pneumoperitoneum:

o   Steady state insufflation rate 200-400mL/min

o   Pressure 10-20mmHg

·         Physiological derangements are caused by:

o   Raised intra-abdominal pressure (↑IAP)

o   Hypercapnoea

o   Positioning

·         Summary of effects:

o   Respiratory: dose-dependent embarrassment (worse if obese or Trendelenburg)

§  Can be offset by recruitment manoeuvres and PEEP, but at expense of CVS system

o   Cardiovascular: variable (worse if hypovolaemic)

 

Intra-abdominal hypertension:

·         Respiratory

o   Cephalad diaphragm displacement

o   ↓Lung volumes

§  ↓FRC ± closing capacity

§  Small airway closure

§  Atelectasis

§  Shunt

§  Hypoxaemia

o   ↓Lung compliance

§  Restrictive deficit

§  ↑Peak airway pressure

§  ↑Risk of barotrauma

§  Hypoventilation

o   Changes worse if obese, pregnant, Trendelenburg

·         Cardiovascular

o   Biphasic effects on venous return

§  At first: autotransfusion of splanchnic blood -> ↑VR -> ↑CO (± initial ↑mAP)

§  Then: compression of IVC -> ↓VR -> ↓CO (more pronounced if hypovolaemic)

o   ↑SVR due to

§  ↑IAP

§  ↑PaCO2 -> ↑catecholamine release

o   Dose-dependent effects on steady-state blood pressure

§  IAP <~20mmHg -> ↑mAP (↑SVR outweighs ↓venous return/cardiac output)

§  IAP >~20mmHg -> ↓mAP (↓venous return/cardiac output outweighs ↑SVR)

o   Effect on myocardial oxygen supply and demand

§  ↑Demand: ↑HR, ↑contractility, ↑stroke work

§  ↑Supply: metabolic autoregulation (but impaired if coronary artery disease)

o   Oliguria

§  ↑IAP ->? renal venous obstruction -> ↓GFR

 

Hypercapnoea:

·         Mechanism:

o   CO2 is rapidly absorbed into systemic circulation (30-50mL/min)

o   Diffuses into rapidly and slowly equilibrating compartments down partial pressure gradient (note large body stores ~120L)

o   CO2 + H2O <-> H2CO3 <-> HCO3- + H+

o   Hence ↑PaCO2 -> ↓pH (whole body including CNS)

o   Prolonged laparoscopy with ↑PaCO2 -> ↑accumulation -> ↑minute ventilation requirement

·         Respiratory

o   Respiratory acidosis

o   Potentiation of HPV

o   Distribution of blood away from poorly-ventilated dependent lung

o   ↑V/Q matching

o   ↑PaO2

o   Hence offsets V/Q inequality caused by small airway closure

·         Cardiovascular

o   If pH >7.2: ↑SNS output -> ↑inotropy

o   If pH <7.2: ↓pH -> ↓inotropy

o   ↓pH -> sensitisation of myocardium to arrhythmogenic effects of catecholamines

o   Pulmonary vasoconstriction

o   Systemic vasodilatation (offsets ↑IAP -> ↑SVR)

o   ↑Myocardial O2 demand (↑HR)

o   ↑Myocardial O2 supply (metabolic autoregulation – but impaired if coronary artery disease)

 

Position:

·         Trendelenburg

o   Respiratory

§  ± Endobronchial intubation -> massive shunt -> ↓PaO2

§  ↑Diaphragm compression

o   Cardiovascular

§  ↑Venous return: offsets venous compression

·         Reverse Trendelenburg

o   Respiratory

§  ↓Diaphragm compression

o   Cardiovascular

§  ↓Venous return: compounds venous compression

 

Miscellaneous complications:

·         Gas in the wrong place:

o   Pneumomediastinum

o   Pneumothorax

o   Subcutaneous emphysema

o   Venous gas embolism -> cardiovascular collapse:

§  Either intravascular insufflation directly into vessel

§  Or Venturi effect

·         Sudden cardiovascular collapse at onset of insufflation

o   Peritoneal stretch -> vagal response

§  Sinus bradycardia, nodal rhythm, asystole)

o   Empty ventricle -> Bezold-Jarisch reflex

§  Contraction of underfilled LV -> stimulation of unmyelinated PSNS C fibres -> ↑↑PSNS output -> ↓HR, ↓BP, coronary vasodilatation

·         Surgical problems

o   Damage to vessel by trochar

o   Difficult to control bleeding

o   Difficult to estimate blood loss
