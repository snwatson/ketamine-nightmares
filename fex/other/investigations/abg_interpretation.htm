ABG interpretation

 

Normal values

·     pH: 7.4

·     PaO2: 100mmHg

·     PaCO2: 40mmHg

·     HCO3: 24mM

·     Anion gap: 16

1.Oxygenation

Absolute:

·     >60mmHg: acceptable

·     ~60mmHg: borderline

·     <60mmHg: hypoxic

P/F ratio in ARDS:

·     Normal: 3.5-5

·     Mild: <3

·     Mod: <2

·     Sev: <1

2.Primary disturbance

·     ↓pH = acidaemia

·     ↑pH = alkalaemia

3.Primary cause

a.Acidaemia:

·     ↑CO2: respiratory acidosis

·     ↓HCO3: metabolic acidosis

b.Alkalaemia:

·     ↓CO2: respiratory alkalosis

·     ↑HCO3: metabolic alkalosis

 

*If combined acidosis or combined alkalosis*

·     The primary disorder is the greater contributor to ∆pH

·     If eyeballing is unhelpful:

o   Acute:    ↑10mmHg CO2 = ↓0.03 pH

o   Chronic: ↑10mmHg CO2 = ↓0.08 pH

4.Compensation

*If right direction = compensation*

*If wrong direction = secondary cause*

 

a.Primary respiratory disorder: “the expected HCO3 is…”

·     Resp acidosis:  ↑10mmHg CO2 = ↑1mM acute, ↑4mM chronic

·     Resp alkalosis: ↓10mmHg CO2 = ↓2mM acute, ↓5mM chronic

b.Primary metabolic disorder: “the expected CO2 is…”

·     Met acidosis: CO2 = 8 + 1.5 x HCO3

·     Met alkalosis: ↑10mM HCO3 = ↑7mmHg CO2 (limited by ↓PaO2)

·     Alternative: CO2 = last two digits of pH

5.Anion gap

Anion gap:

·     Purpose: a) find cause of acidosis b) identify occult HAGMA

·     Calculation: (Na + K) – (HCO3 + HCO3)

·     Correction: ↓10g/L albumin = ↑2.5 anion gap

·     If high: lactate, ketones, poison

·     If normal: normal saline, renal failure, diarrhoea

o   a.k.a. hyperchloraemic acidosis

Delta ratio:

·     Purpose: identify HAGMA + second metabolic disorder

·     Calculation: ∆anion gap / ∆HCO3

·     =1: HAGMA only

·     <1: HAGMA + NAGMA

·     >1: HAGMA + metabolic alkalosis
