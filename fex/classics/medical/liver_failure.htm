Liver failure

 

Pathophysiology:

Synthetic dysfunction

·      Coagulant and anticoagulant factors

·      Impaired drug metabolism

·      Toxin accumulation / encephalopathy

Portal hypertension

·      Portosystemic shunts / variceal bleeding

·      Splenomegaly / platelet sequestration / haemolysis

·      Ascites / respiratory impairment

Other factors

·      Secondary hyperaldosteronism

·      Malnutrition

·      Chronic disease

 

Severity:

Child-Pugh

·      Albumin

·      Bilirubin

·      Coagulation (INR)

·      Distension (ascites)

·      Encephalopathy

Peri-op mortality for major abdo surgery:

·      A: 10%

·      B: 30%

·      C: 80%

But critical care has improved since those studies

MELD

·        Bili

·        INR

·        Creatinine

Better predictor of overall survival

 

Physiological effects:

Overall

·      Very high mortality in major surgery

Airway

·      Impaired gastric emptying

·      Ascites -> abdominal hypertension

Breathing

·      Ascites -> impaired oxygenation and ventilation

·      Hepatopulmonary syndrome / portopulmonary hypertension

Circulation

·      SIRS-like state

·      Reduced RAAS activity

·      Cardiomyopathy

Disability (neuro)

·      Hepatic encephalopathy

·      Hyponatraemia / cerebral oedema

·      Thiamine deficiency / Wernicke’s encephalpathy

·      Susceptibility to anaesthetics and analgesics

·      Prevention: lactulose, rifaximin

Haematological

·      Anaemia

·      Thrombocytopaenia, platelet dysfunction

·      Coagulation and anticoagulation factor deficiency

·      Dysfibrinogenaemia

Immunological

·      Impaired innate and adaptive immunity

·      Susceptibility to SBP

Renal

·      Hepatorenal syndrome

Metabolic

·      Hyperaldosteronism / fluid retention

·      ↓Na, ↓Mg, ↓PO4

·      Hypoglycaemia

·      Hypoproteinaemia / ascites

·      Impaired drug metabolism / detoxicification

 

Pharmacological effects:

Pharmacokinetics

 

-Absorption

·      ↑Oral bioavailability

-Distribution

·      ↓Albumin

·      ↓AAG (not sure)

-Metabolism

·      ↓Phase 1 > ↓Phase 2 reactions

·      ↓Plasma cholinesterase

-Excretion

·      ↓Biliary excretion

Pharmacodynamics

·      ↑Sensitivity to CNS-active drugs

 

Drug choices:

Sedative-hypnotics

·      Halothane -> hepatitis

·      All volatiles -> hapten -> hepatitis (idiosyncratic and rare)

·      Propofol -> maybe easier on the liver

·      Benzodiazepines -> sedation + encephalopathy + slow offset

Local anaesthetics

·      Neuraxial – no

·      Regional – yes

·      Higher risk of LAST

·      Beware high concentration (ropiv >0.25%, bupiv >0.125%)

·      Beware long infusion (>48 hours)

Analgesics

Tricky!

·      Paracetamol – no (hepatotoxicity)

·      NSAID – no (GIT bleed + plt dysfunction + AKI)

·      COX2i – no (AKI)

·      Opioids – beware. Fentanyl and remi ok

·      Ketamine – no (hepatotoxicity, encephalopathy)

Muscle relaxants

·      Benzylisoquinoliniums – preferred

·      Sux – slower offset due to ↓PChE

·      Sugammadex – alters coag profile, probably unimportant

 

Anaesthesia:

Issues

·      A: aspiration

·      B: respiratory failure

·      C: major bleeding

·      D: encephalopathy, pain

·      M: hypoglycaemia, hyponatraemia

Plan

Pre-op:

·      Discussion and planning

·      Drain ascites + conc alb (beware ↓CVP + reaccumulation -> ↓mAP)

Intra-op:

·      Invasive monitoring: A-line, CVC (plus IDC, temp, BIS, NMT)

·      Anticipate bleeding: BGHO, large PIVC, warming

·      Rapid sequence induction

Post-op:

·      HDU/ICU

·      Pain service + FPCA + regional (non-neuraxial)

 

Liver transplant issues:

Pre-anhepatic

·      Not sure

Anhepatic

·      Coagulopathy

·      Hypothermia

·      Hypoglycaemia

Post-hepatic

(reperfusion)

·      Hyperkalaemia: arrhythmias, arrest

·      Acidosis: ↓contractility, ↓SVR, ↓mAP

·      Preparation: volume, pressor, calcium
