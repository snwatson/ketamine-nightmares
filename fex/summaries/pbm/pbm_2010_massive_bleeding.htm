Massive transfusion protocols

 

Damage control resuscitation principles:

1.       Damage control surgery

2.       Permissive hypotension

3.       Haemostatic resus

 

When to call it:

By scenario

·      Anticipate bleeding +++

·      Thorax, abdo, long bone trauma

·      Obstetric bleeding +++

·      GI bleeding +++

By volume

·      50% BV in 4h

·      100% BV in 24h

·      >150mL/min

 

What to do:

1.Activate

·      Call lab

·      Call for help

·      Allocate roles (incl getting, checking, giving blood)

2.Access

·      Big IVs

·      Arterial line

3.Test

·      t = 0 and q30-60

·      ABG, FBE, UEC, CMP, coag, BGHO

·      Viscoelastic if available

4.Stop bleeding

Anaesthesia

·      Permissive hypotension (mAP 50-65, SBP 80-100 unless TBI)

Surgery:

·      Compression/tourniquets/packing

·      Ligation/diathermy/clamping

·      Angiography

Obstetric:

·      Oxytocics

·      Empty the uterus

·      Bakri balloon

·      B-lynch suture

·      Internal iliac artery clamping

·      Hysterectomy

5.Replace blood

·      1st pack: 1:1:1 with TXA

·      2nd pack: 1:1:1 with cryo

·      Cell salvage if available

6.Optimise clotting

Reverse anticoagulation:

·      Warfarin: VitK/PCC/FFP

·      Heparin: protamine

·      Dabigatran: idarucizumab

·      Xa inhibitors: andexanet alpha

·      Everything: ciraparantag

Transfusion strategy:

·      Warmed

·      1:1:1 ratio

·      Limit crystalloid

Substrate targets:

·      Viscoelastic if available

·      plt >80

·      Fg >2

·      INR <1.5

·      aPTT <1.5x control

Supportive targets:

·      Temp >35

·      pH >7.2

·      iCa >1.1

7.Optimise DO2

Targets:

·      PaO2 >100

·      Hb >80

·      PPV <10% (surrogate)

·      pH >7.2

·      Lactate <4

8.Deactivate

·      Call lab when 4,5,6,7 are met
