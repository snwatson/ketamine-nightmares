AHA/ACC Guidelines

 

2020 Valvular heart disease

Assessment

·         History

·         Examination

·         Non-invasive investigations: ECG, TTE, CXR

·         Semi-invasive investigations: CT, MRI

·         Invasive investigations: TOE, catheter

·         MDT for every patient needing surgery

Severity

·         Grades A, B, C, D

o    Symptoms

o    Valve anatomy

o    Valve dysfunction

o    Ventricular dysfunction

o    Upstream complications

§  LV -> PHTN

§  RV -> kidney, liver

Anticoagulation

·         Non-valvular AF:

o    If CHADSVASC ≥2: warfarin or NOAC

o    If HASBLED score high: consider withholding

·         Valvular AF: warfarin

·         Metal mitral valve: warfarin INR 3

·         Metal aortic valve: warfarin INR 2.5

Valve disease

·         Aims:

o    Prevent irreversible damage to ventricles

o    Prevent pulmonary hypertension

o    Prevent irreversible damage to organs

o    Repair vs replacement

·         Aortic stenosis:

o    Symptoms

o    Exercise-induced hypotension

o    LV systolic dysfunction (TTE, BNP)

o    Very severe stenosis

·         Regurgitant valves:

o    Symptoms

o    LV dilation or dysfunction

o    If secondary to ventricular dysfunction: treat that first

o    Better treatments = lower threshold

·         Miscellaneous

o    Mixed valve disease: attend to the worst lesion

o    Note TAVI indications are expanding

Infective endocarditis

·         Blood cultures then antibiotics

·         TTE ±TOE

·         Multi-disciplinary team: surgeon + cardiologist + ID

·         Early surgery if

o    Heart failure

o    Certain pathogens

o    Recurrent emboli or big vegetation

·         Delay surgery >4/52 if

o    Terrible stroke

Pregnancy

·         If severe MS or AS:

o    TAVR pre-conception is ideal

o    TAVR during pregnancy is possible

·         If needing anticoagulation: LMWH with Xa levels

 

2020 Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy

Diagnosis

·         ECG (? Signs)

·         Holter (? Arrhythmia)

·         TTE (? Hypertrophy)

·         TTE + provocation (? LVOTO)

·         MRI (if still unsure)

Treatment

Drugs:

·         B1 blocker

·         Cardiac-selective CCB

·         Disopyramide

AICD if:

·         Malignant arrhythmia

o   Arrest

o   Syncope

o   Sustained VT

·         At risk of malignant arrhythmia

o   Massive LVH >30mm

o   LV dysfunction

o   FHx SCD

Invasive:

·         Alcohol septal ablation

·         Myomectomy

Complicating factors

Ischaemia: do angiogram if

·         Symptoms

·         LV deterioration

·         Planning invasive treatment

AF:

·         Anticoagulate everyone

Pregnant:

·         Ante-natal counselling

·         MDT care

·         Continue beta blocker

·         If anticoagulated: use LMWH

Genetics

·         Patient: gene test

·         First degree relatives: gene test, periodic ECG and TTE

Follow up

·         TTE every 1-2 years, or if deteriorating

 

2019 Primary prevention of heart disease

Lifestyle

·         Proper food

·         Exercise

·         No smoking

Drugs

·         Risk calculator -> consider drug

·         Aspirin: not for everyone

·         Blood pressure: if >130/80

·         Statin: if ↑LDL, T2DM, elderly

 

2018 Pacemakers for conduction delay

SA node

·      SA node scars with age

·      PPM for SA node dysfunction only if symptomatic

·      Nocturnal bradycardias are usually due to OSA -> Sleep study

AV node

·      Second degree AV block Mobitz 2 -> PPM always

·      High grade AV block -> PPM always

·      Other AVs block -> PPM only if symptoms

·      Concomitant heart failure -> Bi-V PPM

·      Concomitant severe heart failure -> Bi-V PPM + AICD (?)

Bundle branches

·      RBBB often benign

·      LBBB often due to structural heart disease -> TTE

 

2018 Adults with congenital heart disease

Severity

·      Grade A, B, C, D

·      Symptoms

·      Exercise tolerance

·      Ventricular function

·      Organ function

·      Arrhythmias

 

·      Lots of generic advice

 

2017 Ventricular arrhythmias

Heart failure

·      LVEF <40: drugs

·      LVEF <35 (non-ischaemic) and NYHA 2-3: AICD

·      LVEF + frequenct monomorphic ectopics: catheter ablation

Ischaemia

·      AMI + sustained VT + failed anti-arrhythmics: ? ablation ? AICD

Other

·      Subcut AICD preferable if not needing pacing

·      Young healthy patients with suspected dangerous arrhythmias need genetic testing, and so do their relatives

·      Don’t insert ICD if short life expectancy

 

2016 Dual antiplatelet therapy

Main thing

·         Stent thrombosis vs major bleeding

Emergency (ACS)

·         DES: 12 months

·         BMS: 12 months

·         No stent: 2 weeks (ideal 12 months)

Elective

·         DES: 6 months

·         BMS: 1 month

Qualifiers

·         Duration can be extended if

o    High DAPT score

o    Low bleeding risk

·         If clopidogrel doesn’t work, use ticagrelor

·         If not stenting, use ticagrelor

 

If planning surgery:

Angioplasty

·      Wait 2/52

Bare metal stent

·      DAPT ideal 6/52

Drug-eluting stent

·      DAPT absolute minimum 3 months

·      Duration dependent on patient, lesion, stent, surgery

·      Discussion: surgeon + cardiologist

Recent AMI

·      Highest risk period <2/12

·      Baseline risk after 6-12/12

 

2016 Heart failure with reduced ejection fraction

 

Renin-angiotensin

·      ACE

·      ARB

·      ARNI = ARB + neprilysin inhibitor

o   Entresto = valsartan + sacubitril

Beta blocker

·      Bisoprolol (B1)

·      Carvedilol (B1, B2, A1)

·      Nebivolol (B1)

·      Metoprolol XR

Mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist

·      Spironolactone

·      Eplerenone

Miscellaneous

·      HCN antagonist (ivabradine): if HR >70

·      SGLT2 inhibitor (-flozin): if T2DM

ICD

·      AICD: if LVEF <35%

·      Bi-V PPM: if LVEF <35% and very wide QRS
