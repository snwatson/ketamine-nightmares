2005B07 Briefly outline the pharmacology of droperidol, emphasising its mechanism of action, peri-operative use and side effects.

 

List:

·      PC

·      PK

·      PD

 

Physicochemical:

Class

·   Butyrophenone

Presentation

·   Aqueous solution

·   Light-protected ampoule

 

Pharmacokinetic:

Administration

·   IV, IM, PO

Time course

·   Onset 5-15 mins

·   Duration: up to 12 hours for sedative effect

Absorption

·   Oral bioav 75%

Distribution

·   Plasma protein binding 85-90%

·   VD 1.5-2.5L/kg

Metabolism

·   Hepatic CYP450

·   Clearance 8mL/kg/min (slow)

·   t1/2β 2 hours

Excretion

·   1% unchanged in urine > faeces

·   Metabolites > urine

 

Pharmacodynamic:

Mechanisms

·   Central DA2 antagonist (Binds Gi GPCR -> ↓cAMP)

·   ↓Afferents from chemoreceptor trigger zone to vomiting centre

·   α-adrenoceptor antagonist

Use (dose)

·   PONV prevention or rescue (0.5-0.625mg in adults)

·   Anti-psychosis, chemical restraint )1.25-2.5mg slowly IV, q10 mins; max 10mg)

·   Neuroleptanaesthesia

Side effects

·   Sedation

·   Mild hypotension

·   EPSE:

o  Parkinsonism (tremor)

o  Akathisia (restlessness)

o  Tardive dyskinesia (rhythmic voluntary movements of the tongue, face and jaw)

o  Acute dystonia (e.g. oculogyric crisis)

·   Neuroleptic malignant syndrome

·   ↑QTC, ↑risk torsades de pointes
