Apical views:

Patient

·     Almost left lateral (moves heart towards probe)

·     Left arm up (opens interspaces)

Probe

·     4th – 6th interspace

·     Between mid-clavicular and mid-axillary lines

·     Dot at 2 o’clock, same degree of rotation as PSAX view

·     Start too low, slide up until apex in view

·     Aim at sternal notch

·     Nestle into the space

Goals

·     Apex at top of screen – thin wall, not moving

·     All chambers visible and as long as possible

·     LV cavity and atrium centered, septum vertical

·     MV and TV visible throughout cardiac cycle

Corrections

·     If apex not centered: slide along the space

·     If apex thick and moving: slide down a space

·     If aorta in view: lift tail up ± rotate anticlockwise

·     If coronary sinus in view: lower tail down

A5C

·     From A4C: drop tail down

·     Should make aorta as long as possible

·     To line up the LVOT: slide along the space

A2C

·     From A4C: rotate anticlockwise, dot points to axilla

·     If lose apex: slide along the interspace

·     If lose atrium: tilt either direction (ideally should see appendage)

·     If RV in view: rotate further anticlockwise

·     If aorta in view: rotate clockwise

A3C

·     From A2C: rotate further anticlockwise, dot points to head

·     To open up LVOT: tilt to patient’s left, rotate either direction
