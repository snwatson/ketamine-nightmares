Other scanning tips:

 

Cardiac cycle

·      End-diastole: peak of R wave (most things) or MV closure (for LVEF)

·      End-systole: end of T wave or smallest LV dimension

Optimising 2D images

·      Depth: object of interest in middle of screen

·      Width: everything important in view, not more, not less

·      Gain: aim for goldilocks brightness. Cavity dark, endocardium defined.

Optimising valve colour doppler

·      Increase sampling rate (Nyquist limit) to maximum

·      Reduce until just before speckling occurs

·      Pan through the valve until maximum regurgitation
(if short axis view: maximum regurg is just below the annulus)

Optimising spectral doppler

·      Ensure flow is in the direction of the beam

·      For LVOT: ensure the clip is in the picture
(I think that’s the case for other measurements near valves too, just can’t remember)

Defining endocardium

·      Follow the most continuous echo line. Ignore the frilly bits.

·      In A4C view, start at the apex then follow the line

Foreshortening

·      In PLAx view: LV appears full of stuff

·      In A4C view: apex is not still

Measuring dimensions

·      For the aorta: leading edge to leading edge (google it)

·      For everything else: it’s intuitive (i.e. cavity size, muscle thickness)

Assessing aorta and pulmonary artery

·      Higher up structures = higher up rib interspaces

Can’t see anything

·      If IPPV: apical and subcostal views

·      Post-sternotomy: apical and subcostal views

·      COPD: apical and subcostal views
