Obstructive sleep apnoea

 

Disease:

Pathophysiology

·      Hypotonia

·      Obstruction

·      Apnoea

·      Hypoxaemia

·      SNS +++

·      Partial arousal

·      Restored patency

Complications

Vessels:

·      Systemic hypertension

·      Pulmonary hypertension

Heart:

·      Arrhythmias (AF)

·      Cor pulmonale

·      Coronary artery disease

Blood:

·      Polycythaemia

·      Stroke

 

Assessment:

Hx/Ex

STOPBANG: (≥3 confers high risk; N.B. high sensitivity)

·      Snoring

·      Tired

·      Observed apnoea

·      Pressure (hypertension)

·      BMI >35

·      Age >50

·      Neck >43cm male, 41cm female

·      Gender (male)

Red flags:

·      Apnoeic period

·      Heroic gasps

·      Daytime somnolence

Risk group:

·      Low: 0-2

·      Intermediate: 3-5

·      High: 6-8

Ix

Blood tests:

·      ↑Hb

·      ↑Hct

Sleep studies:

·      Mild: AHI 5-15

·      Mod: AHI 15-30

·      Sev: AHI >30

TTE:

·      RVSP

·      TAPSE

 

Issues:

Airway

Difficulties:

·      Bag-mask ventilation

·      Intubation (±extubation)

·      (also FONA)

Reasons:

·      If slim: anterior larynx

·      If obese: crowded cavities, difficult positioning

Breathing

Danger:

·      Sedation -> airway obstruction

·      Respiratory depression

·      Highest risk on the first and third nights post-op

Risk factors: = HDU vs continuous oximetry

·      Opioids ++

·      Benzodiazepines

·      Severe OSA (AHI >30 or red flags)

·      Morbidly obese

·      Abnormal upper airway

·      Major comorbidities

Circulation

If pulmonary hypertension:

·      Risk of RV ischaemia

·      Risk of RV failure

 

Anaesthesia:

Intra-op

Anaesthesia:

·      Awake > general > sedation

Airway:

·      Expect difficulty, make plan ABC

·      Oxford ramp

·      HFNP

·      Video laryngoscopy

Post-op

General care:

·      CPAP if prescribed

·      ±Continuous oximetry

·      ±HDU

Analgesia:

·      Prefer regional

·      Prefer non-opioids

·      Prefer atypical to pure opioids

·      Prefer fentanyl PCA to other PCA

·      Avoid benzos

·      Avoid clonidine

 

Notes from QLD presentation:

·        Definitions

o   Apnoea: >10 sec spO2 ↓4%

o   Hypopnoea: 50% reduction in airflow, >10 sec, spO2 ↓4%

·        Complications

o   Resp

o   Cardiac

o   Delirium

o   Mortality probably

·        Other indices of severity

o   Oxygen desaturation index: lasting 6 seconds ↓spo2 4% - how many of those per hour

o   ODI >10 indicates good chance of mod or sev osa

o   Respiratory effort-related arousals: thing that causes either arousal or desaturation without qualifying as apnoea or hypopnea

o   Respiratory disturbance index

·        The highest risk is with undiagnosed = untreated OSA

·        Unknowns

o   How long CPAP for benefit

o   Appropriate form of monitoring

·        Post-op safety

o   O2 supp

o   Direct observation

o   Objective monitoring

o   ↓opioid

o   Non-supine positioning

·        Post-op disposition

o   Low risk: ward

o   Mod risk: ↓opioid and overnight sats

o   High risk: HDU and invasive

·        Low opioid techniques

o   Continuous pulse ox

o   Saturation pattern detection (nellcor)

·        OSA and day surgery

o   Optimised

o   Post-op CPAP is ok

o   Minimal or no opioid

·        Another way of looking at it

o   Care is the same (mnagemnt would not be altered if admitted as inpatient)

o   Will have same risk (being treated as a day case would not alter the per-op risk)

·        Examiners way

o   Severity of osa

o   Type of surgery

o   Type of anaestheisa

o   Post-op analgesia

o   Discharge planning

·        In general

o   <40: automatic acceptance as day surg ?

o   >40: individualized assessment. Refer to anaes beforehand
