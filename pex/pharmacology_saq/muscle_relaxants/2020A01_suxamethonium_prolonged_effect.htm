2020A01 Outline the possible reasons for prolonged paralysis induced by an intravenous dose of 1 mg/kg of suxamethonium.

 

List:

·      Intro

·      Enzyme abnormality (sux apnoea)

·      Enzyme deficiency (phys, path)

·      Enzyme inhibition (drug)

 

Intro:

·         Usual dose 1mg/kg

·         Usual offset 5-10 mins due to high capacity butyrylchoineterase (BChE)

·         Two stage metabolism to choline and succinic acid

 

BChE abnormality (sux apnoea):

Genetics

·   Two copies

·   Four alleles:

o Normal (N)

o Dibucaine-resistance (DR)

o Chloride-resistant (CR)

o Absent (A)

Test

·   Patient’s plasma + dibucaine + 10-5 benzylcholine

·   Dibucaine number = % inhibition of benzylcholine metabolism

·   Normal-normal -> 80% inhibition (as above)

Management

·   If no reversal after 30 mins

·   Prolonged ventilation and sedation in ICU

·   Monitor with nerve stimulator

·   +/- FFP

 

BChE deficiency (rarely significant):

Physiological causes

·   Neonate -> ↓production

·   Pregnancy -> ↑ECF water -> same amount but ↓concentration

Pathological causes

·   Heart failure

·   Liver failure

·   Renal failure

·   Thyrotoxicosis

·   Cancer

 

BChE inhibition (rarely significant):

·         Acetylcholinesterase inhibitors e.g. neostigmine

·         Metoclopramide

·         Ketamine

·         Oral contraceptive

·         Lithium

·         Lignocaine

·         Ester local anaesthetics

·         Cyclophosphamide
