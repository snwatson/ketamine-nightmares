ABG interpretation

 

1.Oxygenation

·     Normal: P/F ratio 3.5-5 (assuming normal CO2)

·     Hypoxia: <60mmHg

2.Primary disturbance

·     ↓pH = acidaemia

·     ↑pH = alkalaemia

3.Primary cause

a.Acidaemia:

·     ↑CO2: respiratory acidosis

·     ↓CO2: metabolic alkalosis

b.Alkalaemia:

·     ↓CO2: respiratory alkalosis

·     ↑CO2: metabolic alkalosis

4.Compensation

*If expected direction: = compensation*

*If wrong direction = secondary cause*

 

a.Respiratory disorder: “the expected HCO3 is…”

·     Resp acidosis:  ↑10mmHg CO2 = ↑1mM acute, ↑4mM chronic

·     Resp alkalosis: ↓10mmHg CO2 = ↓2mM acute, ↓5mM chronic

b.Metabolic disorder: “the expected CO2 is…”

·     Met acidosis: CO2 = last two digits of pH

·     Met alkalosis: ↑1mM HCO3 = ↑0.7mmHg (limited)

5.Fancy

*If metabolic acidosis*

-> Anion gap:

·     Calculation: (Na + K) – (HCO3 + HCO3) = 16

·     Correction: ↓10g/L albumin = ↑2.5 anion gap

·     If high: lactate, ketones, poison

·     If normal: normal saline, renal failure, diarrhoea

 

*If high anion gap*

-> Delta ratio:

·     i.e. ∆anion gap / ∆HCO3

·     >1: concurrent metabolic alkalosis

·     <1: concurrent NAGMA
