Parasternal long axis:

 

How to:

Video link

03. Parasternal Long Axis View - YouTube

05. Right ventricular inflow & outflow views - YouTube

Patient

·     Almost left lateral (move heart towards probe)

·     Left arm up (opens interspaces)

Probe

Standard view:

·     Left sternal edge

·     3rd interspace

·     Dot towards right shoulder

Other views:

·     RVIT: lower interspace, tilt away from left shoulder, ±rotate anticlockwise

·     RV outflow: higher interspace, tilt towards left shoulder, ±rotate clockwise

·     For aorta: higher interspace, rock towards head

Goals

·     Wide open LV cavity (apex not visible)

·     Ventricular walls horizontal

·     MV leaflets in middle of screen

·     AV leaflets meeting symmetrically in middle of lumen

Corrections

·     If LV not open: rotate until parallel to the long axis; then tilt either way

·     If LV walls not horizontal: slide to higher interspace

·     If apex in view: rock or slide towards sternum

·     If AV cusps not symmetric: rotate or tilt in either direction

 

LV inflow-outflow view:

Structure

Eyeball

·      LV cavity size

·      LV wall thickness

·      1/3 RVOT, 1/3 LVOT, 1/3 LA

·      Pericardial space (in front of desc aorta)

·      Pleural space (behind desc aorta)

Measurements

·      RVOT (orthogonal to septum)

·      LA (end-systole, orthogonal to Ao long axis)

·      LV cavity (at MV tips, parallel to annulus)

·      LV wall thickness (AS & IL walls)

·      LVOT (5mm from annulus, mid-systole)

·      Aortic annulus (at cusp insertion)

·      Aortic sinus ± STJ

Function

Eyeball

·      LV anteroseptal and inferolateral walls

Measurements

·      FS

Valves

Eyeball

·      Pan through

·      Open and shut?

·      Valves normal?

·      Annulus normal?

Colour doppler

·      MV

·      AV/LVOT

 

Minor views:

RV inflow

·      RV: eyeball

·      TV: eyeball and colour doppler

·      PA pressure: CWD through TR jet (max velocity -> 4v2 + RAP)

RV outflow

·      PV: eyeball, colour doppler

·      PA pressure: PWD at PV (PVAT i.e. time to peak systolic velocity)
