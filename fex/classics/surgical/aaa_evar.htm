EVAR:

 

General issues:

Patient selection

·     Ideal if very old, very comorbid

·     Lower early mortality rate

·     Same 2 year mortality rate

·     Endoleak very common after a few years

Difficulty

·     Duration highly variable

·     Conversion to open 1/100

Complications

·     Renal failure (contrast ++)

·     Spinal cord infarction

·     Endoleak (very common after a few years)

Surgical conditions

Require immobility + periodic apnoea

·     If <2h: spinal + sedation

·     If >2h: relaxant GA

Anticoagulation

·     Initiation: heparin e.g. 5000 units

·     Monitoring: ACT

·     Reversal: protamine (rarely at St V)

 

Organ protection

 

Kidneys:

Risk factors

·     Patient: CKD, heart failure, T2DM, renovascular disease

·     Surgery: recent contrast, long clamp time, renal artery disruption

·     Anaesthesia: hypoperfusion, nephrotoxins, massive transfusion

Preserve vessels

·     Fenestrated graft

·     Re-anastomosis (if open)

Preserve perfusion

·     ↑mAP: volume + pressor

·     ↓CVP: cardiac function

Things that don’t work

·     Dopamine (renal dose 1-3mcg/kg/min)

·     Mannitol

·     Frusemide

·     NAC

·     Methylprednisolone

 

Spinal cord:

Risk factors

·     Patient: not sure

·     Surgery: affects lumbar arteries, artery of Adamkiewicz

·     Anaesthesia: hypoperfusion

Monitoring

·     MEP and SSEP

Preserve vessels

·     Fenestrated graft

·     Re-anastomosis (if open)

Preserve perfusion

·     ↑mAP (aim 90mmHg): volume + pressor

·     ↓CVP: cardiac function

·     ↓CSF pressure: lumbar drain

o   Indicated if high risk (e.g. complex TEVAR)

o   Transducer at level of insertion

o   Max 10cmH2O

o   Max 30mL/h drainage (risk of SDH, coning)

o   Keep in situ 72 hours (high risk period)

o   Monitor in ICU

o   Prophylactic antibiotics
