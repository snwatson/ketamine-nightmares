Why this article?

Anaemia common

In major elective surgery patients:

·      33% anaemic

·      20% not anaemic but iron deficient

Anaemia bad

·      ↑Infection

·      ↑Thrombotic events

·      ↑Blood transfusion

·      ↑Mortality

(even iron deficiency without anaemia is bad)

Anaemia fixable

·      Iron

·      Vit B12

·      Folate

·      Epo

Blood transfusion bad

·      Adverse reactions

·      Wound infection

·      Pneumonia

·      Sepsis

·      Cardiac event

·      Death

 

Anaemia

Definition

·      <130 men

·      <120 women

·      <110 pregnant

Common causes

·      Absolute iron deficiency

·      Functional iron deficiency

·      Reduced response to Epo

Physiology of iron

·      Absorbed in duodenum

·      Stored as ferritin in enterocytes, macrophages, hepatocytes

·      Exported by ferroportin in absence of hepcidin

·      Circulates bound to transferrin, available for erythropoiesis

Pathophysiology of functional iron deficiency

·      Systemic inflammation or organ dysfunction

·      ↑Hepcidin, ↑Ferritin

·      ↓Absorption, ↓release, ↑sequestering

·      Hence iron unavailable for erythropoiesis

Blood tests suggestive of iron deficiency

·      Ferritin <30   OR   30-100 with ↑CRP

·      Transferrin saturation <20%

·      ↓MCV

·      ↑RCDW

·      ↓Reticulocyte Hb

 

Investigation of anaemia

Erythrocytes

·      MCV

·      RCDW

·      MCH

·      Reticulocyte count

·      Reticulocyte Hb

Haematinics

·      Iron

·      Ferritin

·      Transferrin saturation

·      B12

·      Folate

Organs

·      UEC

·      LFT

·      TFT

·      Bone marrow biopsy

Inflammation

·      CRP

Referral

·      Gastro (if needing scopes)

·      Haem (if not iron deficiency or chronic disease)

·      Renal (if needing Epo)
