PS15 2018: Day surgery

 

List:

·         Patient

·         Operation

·         Facility

·         Discharge criteria

 

Premise:

·         Trend towards more day surgery, even if major surgery and major comorbidities

·         Need to ensure patients receive adequate care

·         Decision to go ahead falls to the anaesthetist

 

Suitable for day surgery?

1.       Patient

2.       Operation

3.       Facility

 

1.Patient:

ASA

·      1-2 or medically stable ASA 3-4 (but this alone is not enough)

Obesity

·      BMI <35 (negotiable)

Airway

·      Expected to be easy

Sleep-disordered breathing

·      Not severe

·      Will require minimal opioid

Children

·      Age limit dependent upon

·      Location

·      Operation

·      Staff experience

·      Watch out if young and premature

 

2.Operation:

Airway

·      Will be safe

Bleeding

·      Will be minimal

Pain

·      Will need only oral analgesics

Eating and drinking

·      Can do so straight away

General care

·      Can be done by patient or helper or district nurse

Recovery time

·      First on the list if needing all day

 

3a.Facility: non-negotiable

Patient care

·      Staff qualified and sufficient in number

·      Equipment sufficient (including resuscitation)

·      Infection control attended to

·      Restricted drugs handled properly

Patient flow

·      Pre-admission: robust screening for suitability and referral to anaes clinic

·      Recovery: both first and second stage areas

·      Discharge planning: system to arrange overnight stay if needed

Practical

·      Easy access for Ambulance

·      Wheelchair accessible

·      Nearby parking

Legal

·      Licensed

·      Outcomes audited

·      Adverse events documented and reported

 

3b.Facility: keep these things in mind

·         Hospital-linked vs free-standing

·         Urban vs rural

 

Discharge criteria:

Vital signs

·      Normal

Conscious state

·      Normal

Mobility

·      Able to get around

Pain

·      Under control with oral

·      Script provided if needed

Nausea

·      Under control with orals

·      Script provided if needed

Eating and drinking

·      Enough

Urine

·      Passed urine if at risk of retention

Wound/drain

·      Minimal bleeding

Instructions

·      Written and verbal

·      Regular and new meds

·      Activity restrictions

·      Return advice

Companion

·      To take patient home

·      Stay the night

Transport

·      Car or taxi

·      Not public transport
