Doppler

 

Doppler effect:

Definition

·  Apparent frequency ↑/↓if object is moving towards/away from probe

Use

·  Demonstrate flow and direction of flow

·  Calculate velocity (e.g. aortic jet)

·  Calculate distance by integration (e.g. VTI)

Equation

V = ∆F x C /2F0cosθ

·  V: velocity

·  ∆F: change in frequency between emission and detection

·  C: speed of sound

·  2x: ultrasound wave does a return trip

·  F0: emitted frequency

·  cosθ: correction for incident angle
N.B. ultrasound does not correct for angle.
Need <20° angle for <6% inaccuracy.

Modes

·  Colour mode: recognize abnormal motion (e.g. valvular regurgitation)

·  Pulse wave: specified depth, low velocity (e.g. LVOT VTI)

·  Continuous wave: unspecified depth, high velocity (e.g. LVOT PSV)

 

Doppler imaging:

Colour

·  Blue away, red towards

·  Use: detection of abnormal motion or direction; PISA calculations

Pulse wave

·  Ultrasound emission: intermittent pulses

·  Cursor: gate (specified latency = specified depth)

·  Displayed waveform: single band (± range ambiguity artifact)

·  Detectable velocity: low (first pulse must return before second is sent)

·  Item of interest: velocity at the gate (e.g. LVOT)

·  If velocity too high: aliasing (I call it decapitation artifact)
(More likely to occur if distant object since longer travel time)

Continuous wave

·  Emissions

·  Cursor: line (any latency = any depth)

·  Displayed waveform: filled-in

·  Detectable velocity: high (near continuous emission and detection)

·  Item of interest: maximum velocity along the dotted line (e.g. tight valve)

HPRF mode

·  High pulse repetition frequency

·  Cross between continuous wave and pulse wave

·  Second pulse sent out before the first has returned

·  Cursor: two locations

·  Displayed waveform: two bands (i.e. downside is range ambiguity)

Nyquist limit

·  What: the frequency below which aliasing occurs

·  Why: must sample ≥2 points on each oscillation to determine frequency

·  Calc: limit = 2 x shifted frequency (i.e. the frequency detected, not emitted)
